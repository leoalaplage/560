QUALITY INVESTING


Quality investing stands out for its ability to deliver exponential growth in free cash flow through companies known as compounders. These companies possess a combination of favorable characteristics that drive sustainable growth. Once a company is thoroughly researched and correctly assessed, it can be relied upon to work and compound investments over time. This approach aligns perfectly with a buy-and-hold strategy, making quality investing unique.


In contrast, value investing involves purchasing undervalued companies and selling them once their stock prices reach their intrinsic value. This requires a continuous search for new undervalued opportunities. With quality investing, however, it is possible to buy exceptional companies at fair prices and hold onto them indefinitely.


The crucial question is how to discover these high-quality companies. Typically, such companies exhibit certain key traits


- A STRONG COMPETITIVE EDGE (WIDE MOAT)

- TRUSTWORTHY MANAGEMENT

- LOW CAPITAL REQUIREMENTS

- EFFECTIVE CAPITAL ALLOCATION

- HIGH PROFITABILITY

- STRONG HISTORICAL GROWTH


By identifying these characteristics, L8 TRUST selects companies that are well-positioned for sustained success.

TRUSTWORTY MANAGEMENT


We diligently seek out companies led by management teams with high integrity, whose interests are intricately aligned with those of our investors. It 's paramount to us that the management holds a significant personal stake in the company 's success.


This alignment of interests underscores why family-owned businesses often outperform their non-family counterparts. Their unwavering commitment and vested interest typically drive superior performance and long-term growth, positioning them for sustained success.


WIDE MOAT (COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE)


L8 TRUST aims to invest in companies that have already proven their dominance. Instead of chasing emerging trends, we target established market leaders with robust pricing power. These are companies that have consistently outperformed the market over many years, sometimes even decades, showcasing their sustained success and competitive strength.

LOW CAPITAL REQUIREMENTS


We advocate for a focus on businesses that demand minimal capital to sustain and grow. These companies require little investment to maintain operations and expand.


Identifying firms with low capital intensity, high profitability, and the capability to reinvest significantly in growth opportunities is paramount for quality investors like us. By doing so, we effectively invest in powerful compounding machines, positioning ourselves for sustained success.

HIGH PROFITABILITY


We emphasize the importance of high profitability, measured by the free cash flow (FCF) margin, which illustrates the percentage of sales that convert into cash.


Typically, companies utilize their FCF for reinvestment, share buybacks, acquisitions, and dividends.


We regard free cash flow as more significant than earnings because it accurately reflects the actual cash influx into the business, providing a clearer picture of a company 's financial health and growth potential.


EFFECTIVE CAPITAL ALLOCATION


At L8 TRUST , we understand the critical importance of effective capital allocation in driving long-term growth and value creation for investors.


When a company generates cash flow, it has three options for allocating its free cash flow (FCF): reinvesting in the business, acquisitions/buybacks, and dividends.


For quality investors like us, reinvesting in the business is the preferred choice. We prioritize investing in companies that can efficiently channel a significant portion of their FCF into future growth opportunities at high profitability rates and favorable capital allocation metrics.


Additionally, acquisitions and buybacks represent another avenue for utilizing FCF. Companies may choose to repurchase their own shares, effectively increasing FCF per share as shares are retired, or pursue acquisitions to expand their operations.


While dividends provide income, they are generally considered less efficient for capital allocation as they do not fuel future growth, and investors are subject to taxes on the dividends received.

STRONG HISTORICAL GROWTH


In our approach, we place great emphasis on understanding market trends in investment decisions. Investing in companies aligned with strong secular trends is essential. Consider themes such as urbanization, cybersecurity, data centers, semiconductors, hearing aids, and obesity. We focus on companies operating in rapidly expanding markets, where there is substantial potential to gain market share within this growth trajectory.

CLOSING NOTES


In our investment approach, we aim to invest in the world 's leading companies, prioritizing proven winners over fleeting trends. Assessing a company 's stock performance over years, even decades, provides valuable insights into its success trajectory.


Key characteristics of quality companies include a wide economic moat, strong management integrity, low capital intensity, effective capital allocation, high profitability, impressive historical growth, and alignment with strong secular trends. When these companies are acquired at fair valuations, they serve as potent compounding machines, delivering exceptional results over time.
